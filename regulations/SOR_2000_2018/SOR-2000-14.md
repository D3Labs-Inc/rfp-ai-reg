# Summary
NATIONAL DEFENCE ACT Military Police Professional Code of Conduct.
A member of the military police is presumed to discredit the military police if the member is convicted of an offence under the Act, other than an offence tried at a summary trial for which a minor punishment or a fine of not more than 25% of basic monthly pay has been imposed; or an indictable offence, or an offence punishable on summary conviction, under any other Act of Parliament or the legislature of a province.
A member of the military police shall report to their superior in the military police chain of command any incident for which they have been charged with an offence under the Act or any other Act of Parliament or the legislature of a province.
A response given by a member of the military police under subsection (1) shall not be used against the interests of the member by the Military Police Credentials Review Board established under Chapter 22 of the  Queen’s Regulations and Orders for the Canadian Forces .
