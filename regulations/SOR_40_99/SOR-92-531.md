# Summary
BANK ACT Regulations Defining the Regulatory Capital of a Bank.
Subject to subsection (2) and section 4, the regulatory capital of a bank, at a particular time, is the aggregate of the amounts of shareholders equity, members’ equity, if the bank is a federal credit union, minority interests, and subordinated indebtedness that would be reported in the financial statements of the bank prepared as at that time in accordance with the accounting principles and specifications of the Superintendent referred to in subsection 308(4) of the Act, less the amount of goodwill that would be included in those financial statements.
